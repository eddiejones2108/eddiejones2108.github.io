On the Success of Category Theory

Category Theory promises a lot, but does mathematics (relatively) new superstar have a less glamerous underbelly?

The leap from Set Theory to Category Theory, is a shift in focus. Set theory studies objects and collections. Although category theory can express these concepts, it's all about the structure these objects have and consequently operations, or morphisms that preserve or define that structure.
Without a constant misue of language there cannot be any discovery, any progress
Prehaps precision can actually get in the way? Should we reinvoke the ancient intuitive style of mathematics?
